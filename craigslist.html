Craigslist Location Selection

Keywords: HCI, UX design, HTML, CSS, Javascript, search, filtering, empirical evaluation

In Fall 2021, I took CS6750 at Georgia Tech, which is Human Computer Interaction. In this class, we spent the semester learning and applying principles and methods of human computer interaction to a problem space of our choice. As I am an avid user of Craigslist and move a lot (thus change my Craiglist location all the time), I wanted to improve the user experience associated with selecting a location to filter content.

Throughout the semester, I investigated the problem space, conducted needfinding exercises, defined user requirements, and formed several potential prototypes. I then selected a searchbar with a dropdown as the prototype and created an empirical performance study to determine whether this prototype improved the speed and efficiency that a user would need to specify a location. It proved successful.
