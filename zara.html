Zara Content Filtering

Keywords: HCI, UX design, search, filtering, qualitative evaluation, InVision

In Fall 2021, I took CS6750 at Georgia Tech, which is Human Computer Interaction. In this class, we spent the semester learning principles and methods of human computer interaction. At the end of the semester, we applied what we learned to a problem space of our choice in a final project. I wanted to improve the user experience associated with filtering content on Zara's website.

Over four weeks, I investigated the problem space, conducted needfinding exercises, defined user requirements, and formed a high fidelity card prototype (as seen above). I then performed 4 interviews to determine if this prototype increased the usability and decreased the cognitive load to improve the average user's experience on Zara's website. It proved somewhat successful, with several takeaways for future refinements.
